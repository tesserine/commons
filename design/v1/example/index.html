Tesserine Design System

Specimen surface — a projection of the design/v1 member files, version 1.0.0.

Every Tesserine surface is a typeset record: the quiet, documentary projection of a machine-readable artifact that remains the product.

The typeset record

Entry — running matter, structure, and state, all drawn from the members.

This page is the system's worked consumption example, and its content is the system's own record. Every color, size, space, face, and section form on this surface is a named member of the declared set — the page consumes by reference only, and the closure assay fails it the moment any value appears here that the members do not declare. The members themselves live in the token set, the type scale, and the section grammar; their derivations from the governing fact are stated in SYSTEM.md.

Ink speaks only for the record

Color departs from ink solely to carry the record's own semantics. A record's states render as passing, needs attention, and failing; a citation renders in the reference ink, as the links above do. No other departure from ink exists on this page, because no other departure is declared.

One rhythm, one ratio

Every interval on this page is a named fraction or multiple of the reading line, and every text size — from this running matter up through the headings above it to the masthead — is one step of the single modular ratio. The scale's steps rank the record's structure; nothing on the page shouts.
